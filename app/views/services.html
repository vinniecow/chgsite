Our Services

Working with Congress

The CHG analyzes pending legislation that could favorably or negatively impact funding or policy for clients. We develop positions on legislation and implementation strategies to be communicated to delegation staff, appropriations and authorizing committee staff, and to Members, either in writing, in person, over the phone, or by oral testimony. CHG helps prepare policy requests for your Members of Congress and appropriate congressional committees, including but not limited to increasing funding for the Bus and Bus Facilities program, expanding use of Federal funds for operating assistance, and streamlining the Federal Transit Administration’s (FTA) Small Starts process. The firm will connect CAT to key lawmakers representing interests across the country and in particular will work closely with the Georgia delegation, including Senators Isakson and Perdue, Representative Carter, and their DC and district staff. These connections will be enhanced by the fact that for over 15 years, Ms. Graves has maintained strong relationships with transportation authorizers and appropriators and their staff in both the House and Senate.

Working with the Administration and Federal Agencies

CHG assists in obtaining and expediting Federal grants. Working in conjunction with Federal agencies, the firm advises clients on how to satisfy planning, procurement, environmental, Buy America, labor, ADA, and other Federal grant requirements. The firms also provide technical and management advice during all phases of project development, helping navigate complex Federal processes, educating and advising local public officials, local boards/councils and client staff. The firm will helps maintain and advance relationships with key Federal agency staff at both the regional and headquarters level

Promoting Client Priorities and Monitoring Activities in Washington

CHG continuously monitors developments in Washington, DC that are important to clients, communicates significant proposed actions to clients, and helps develop and communicate client positions to congressional and Administration staff and principals. We maintain on-going contact with Federal agencies and committees that correspond to the Federal agenda and remain alert to all areas which could have a beneficial or detrimental impact on clients. When clients travel to Washington, we coordinate all Washington activities; CHG schedules and prepares clients for meetings with their congressional delegation, authorizing and appropriations committee chairs and staff, senior executive and legislative branch officials and their State office, as appropriate. CHG communicates with clients on a daily, weekly and monthly basis, as appropriate, on the results of our efforts to:

  • Monitor legislation and agency rulemaking
  • Identify ongoing or discretionary grant opportunities, including opportunities such as TIGER grants, and assist CAT staff prepare grant applications
  • Participate in coalition meetings
  • Attend Congressional mark-ups and hearings
  • Meet with congressional and committee offices, Federal agency and Administration officials in order to advance client’s interests
  • Represent clients at public interest group meetings in DC, and
  • Track pertinent Federal policy and monitor appropriations and authorizing legislation with regular reports to clients.

Drafting and Research

CHG provide technical and strategic help to clients and delegation staff to draft any needed legislative language, congressional testimony, floor statements, and letters, and to offer advice on how to respond to requests for information. In addition to helping with any written briefing materials, we would also assist in preparing and presenting oral communications, whether they are formal testimony before committees or informal briefings with small groups or individuals. We will serve as an extension of client staff, as well as that of the Members’ staff, ensuring they have the research, legislative, and technical support, and staffing resources necessary.
